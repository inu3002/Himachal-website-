Mini History of Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is a beautiful hill state of northern India. The name Himachal comes from two Sanskrit words: Hima (snow) and Achal (land), meaning "Land of Snow".

In ancient times, this region was known as Dev Bhoomi (Land of Gods). Many sages, saints, and civilizations lived and meditated in these mountains. Before independence, the region was ruled by several small hill kingdoms and later came under British rule.

Himachal Pradesh became a full state of India on 25 January 1971. The state has a unique capital system: Shimla is the summer capital, while Dharamshala, located in Kangra district, serves as the winter capital.

Dharamshala is internationally famous as the residence of His Holiness the 14th Dalai Lama and is a major center of Tibetan culture, Buddhism, education, and tourism.

Today, Himachal Pradesh is widely known for its breathtaking natural beauty, temples, rivers, snow-covered mountains, trekking routes, adventure sports, rich culture, and deep spiritual heritage.

Natural Beauty
